# Hilbert-Schmidt

## general situation

For any operators, there are

$$
F = \sum_i tr(R_i^\dagger F) R_i,
$$

where HS basic meet

$$
\begin{cases}
tr(R_i^\dagger R_j) = \delta_{ij}\\
R_{i, b}^{a*}R_{i, d}^c = \delta_{ac}\delta_{bd}
\end{cases}
$$

There respectively demonstrate the **orthogonality and completeness** of the matrix basis.

## break Hermitian

If 

$$
F = F^\dagger
$$

there are

$$
F = \sum_i tr(R^\dagger_i F) \hat R_i = \sum_i tr(R_iF)\hat R_i
$$
## Pauil basics

There are

$$
R_\mu \in \{\frac{1}{\sqrt 2}(I, X, Y, Z)\}^{\otimes N},
$$

since

$$
R_\mu^\dagger = R_\mu
$$

Viz.,

$$
\begin{cases}
tr(R_i R_j) = \delta_{ij}\\
R_{i, b}^{a}R_{i, d}^c = \delta_{ad}\delta_{bc}\\
F = \sum_i tr(R_i F) R_i
\end{cases}
$$
