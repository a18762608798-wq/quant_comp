Known

$$
\hat \rho = \mathbb E_{U, s} [\widetilde{\hat \rho}] = \mathbb E_{U} [\sum_{s} tr(\hat P(U. s) \hat \rho)  \widetilde{\hat \rho}],
$$

writen as a linear transformation, which is always established for any state of single bit.

$$
\hat \rho = \tau(\hat \rho). 
$$

Also

$$
\hat \rho = \sum\limits_{R} tr(\hat R \hat \rho)\hat R,
$$

Viz.,

$$
\tau(\hat \rho) = \sum_R tr(\hat R\hat \rho) \tau(\hat R) = \hat \rho = \sum_R tr(\hat R\hat \rho) \hat R \Rightarrow \tau(\hat R) = \hat R.
$$

Therefore for any operator of single bit

$$
\tau(\hat A) = \hat A
$$
